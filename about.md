## Engineering Philosophy

### 1. Offline & Private AI (Edge Computing)
In an era dominated by large cloud service platforms, Mohamed believes in local, private, and offline computational intelligence. By running neural networks directly on microcontrollers and embedded devices (TinyML), systems achieve zero-latency response, function without network connectivity, and preserve complete data privacy.

### 2. Full-Stack Prototyping
Building intelligent systems requires control over both software weights and physical mechanics. Mohamed operates a personal laboratory featuring self-assembled FDM 3D printers and home electrochemistry tanks for electroless copper plating of double-sided through-hole PCBs. This enables rapid, professional mechanical and electrical prototyping of custom wearable systems, prosthetic limbs, and smart home automation devices.

### 3. Distributed Compute Architectures
When local microchips face computational resource bounds, Mohamed researches decentralized token-routing pipelines. By splitting inference parameters and distributing token generation tasks to client pools over WebSocket networks, developers can utilize pooled community hardware resources (Swarm Groups) to speed up execution.
