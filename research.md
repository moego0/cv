# Research of Mohamed Ahmed Abdelraouf

Academic investigations and personal technical research program operated at the Mohamed AI Laboratory.

## Core Research Areas

### 1. TinyML & Edge Optimization
- **Goal**: Running high-accuracy neural network classifiers on edge microcontrollers under tight static memory (<100KB RAM) limits.
- **Methods**: Exploring INT8 and INT4 post-training quantization, pruning convolutional layers, optimization of Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCC) feature extraction, and assembly compiler optimizations for ARM Cortex-M microchips.
- **Applications**: Keyword spotting (Wake Word Engine), real-time biomedical sensor-fusion stress classifiers.

### 2. Client-Side Distributed Compute (Swarm Networks)
- **Goal**: Executing Large Language Model (LLM) inference and planning agents locally on pooled developer machines.
- **Methods**: Developing lightweight WebSocket servers and client bridges that split token generation passes across local subnetwork machines (Swarm Groups).
- **Applications**: Repository-aware planning assistants (Local Tab Agent).

### 3. Biological Signal Processing
- **Goal**: Translating skin electrode potential changes into stable, proportional robotic motor actuations.
- **Methods**: Differential instrumentation amplification, active full-wave rectification filtering, and digital exponential moving average (EMA) envelope extraction in embedded C loops.
- **Applications**: Electromyography (EMG) controlled bionic hand prosthetics.

### 4. Hardware Kinematics and Electrochemistry Prototyping
- **Goal**: Prototyping multi-layer electronic boards and structures at home.
- **Methods**: CoreXY motion configuration, Marlin firmware configurations, palladium chloride sensitization, electroless copper-deposition, and copper sulfate electroplating of vias.
- **Applications**: Custom sensor wristbands, mechanical mechanical hands, and macro shortcut keypads.
